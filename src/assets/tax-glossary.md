# Income Glossary

## Earned Income
Money you receive **because you worked, ran a business, or provided a service**.

---

### Trade, Business, or Profession
Income earned from **running your own business or professional practice**.

**Example:**
A mechanic running their own store and making ₦3,000,000 profit in a year.

---

### Employment
Money earned as **salary or wages from an employer**.

**Example:**
A software developer earning ₦500,000 monthly from a company.

---

### Consultancy / Professional Fees
Money earned by **providing expert services or advice**, usually on a contract or freelance basis.

**Example:**
A developer paid ₦800,000 to build a website for a company.

---

## Other Income

### Director Fees
Payment received for **serving on the board of a company**.

**Example:**
A board member paid ₦1,500,000 yearly for attending board meetings.

---

### Benefit in Kind (BIK)
**Non-cash benefits** provided by an employer.

**Example:**
A company gives an employee a **car, accommodation, or paid driver**.

---

### Terminal Bonus
A **final bonus payment when leaving a job**.

**Example:**
An employee receives ₦1,000,000 when resigning after several years of service.

---

### Contract Income
Money earned from **executing a contract or completing a project**.

**Example:**
A contractor paid ₦2,000,000 to renovate a building.

---

### Pension
Regular payments received **after retirement**.

**Example:**
A retired civil servant receiving ₦75,000 monthly pension.

---

### Allowances
Extra payments given **in addition to salary**.

**Example:**
Transport allowance, housing allowance, or meal allowance.

---

### Profit Share
Money received as **a share of profits from a business or partnership**.

**Example:**
You invest in a friend's restaurant and receive ₦300,000 as your share of the profit.

---

### Commission
Payment earned when you **sell a product or service**.

**Example:**
A real estate agent earning ₦500,000 commission after selling a house.

---

## Unearned Income
Money received **without actively working for it**, usually from investments or assets.

---

### Dividend
Money paid to shareholders from a company's profits. This will be updated at Dividend (FII) in the Deductions section.

**Example:**
You receive ₦50,000 dividend from shares in **Dangote Cement**.

---

### Interest
Money earned from **savings, loans, or investments**. This will be updated at Interest (FII) in the Deductions section.

**Example:**
Interest earned from a bank savings account or treasury bills.

---

### Royalty
Payment received when **others use your intellectual property**.

**Example:**
A musician earns royalties when their songs are streamed, such as music by **Burna Boy**.

---

### Rent
Money earned by **leasing property to others**.

**Example:**
A landlord renting out a flat for ₦2,000,000 per year.

---

# Deductions
Deductions are **amounts subtracted from your total income before calculating tax**, usually because they are **tax-exempt, already taxed, or allowed as reliefs**.

---

## FII Income
**Franked Investment Income (FII)** refers to **investment income that has already had tax paid at the company level**, so it may not be taxed again in the same way for the individual.

---

### Interest (FII)
Interest earned from certain investments that may already have **withholding tax deducted**.

**Example:**
You earn **₦50,000 interest** from treasury bills or fixed deposits, and the bank already deducted withholding tax.

---

### Dividend (FII)
Dividends received from companies where **tax has already been paid on the company's profits**.

**Example:**
You own shares in **Dangote Cement** and receive **₦60,000 dividend**.
The company already paid corporate tax before distributing the dividend.

---

## Other Deductions

### Pensions
Contributions made to a **retirement savings account (RSA)** or pension fund that reduce taxable income.

**Example:**
If you earn ₦400,000 monthly and contribute **₦32,000 to pension**, that amount may be deducted before tax is calculated.

---

### Gratuities
A **lump sum payment received after retirement or long service**, which may be exempt or partially exempt from tax.

**Example:**
After 25 years at a company, you receive **₦5,000,000 gratuity**.

---

### Bonus
Additional payments given by an employer **on top of salary**, sometimes eligible for special tax treatment depending on the system.

**Example:**
An employee receives **₦500,000 end-of-year bonus** for meeting targets.

---

### Other Deductions
Any **other approved deductions or tax reliefs** that reduce taxable income.

**Example:**
Certain approved contributions, adjustments, or tax reliefs allowed by tax regulations.

---

# Adjustments
Adjustments are **changes made to your income before tax is calculated**.
They usually account for **business losses or the cost of assets used for business**.

---

### Balancing Charge
An **additional taxable amount added to your income** when you sell a business asset for **more than its remaining tax value**.

**Example:**
You bought a delivery van for **₦2,000,000** for your business.
After claiming depreciation over time, its remaining tax value becomes **₦800,000**.
If you sell the van for **₦1,200,000**, the extra **₦400,000** may be added to your income as a **balancing charge**.

---

### Balancing Allowance
A **tax deduction given when you sell a business asset for less than its remaining tax value**.

**Example:**
You bought a refrigerator for **₦1,000,000**.
After depreciation, its remaining tax value becomes **₦500,000**.
If you sell it for **₦300,000**, the difference **₦200,000** can be claimed as a **balancing allowance (a deduction)**.

---

### Losses
Business losses that can **reduce your taxable income**.

**Example:**
Your small business spends **₦4,000,000** in a year but only earns **₦3,000,000**.
Your **₦1,000,000 loss** can reduce your taxable income.

---

### Capital Allowance
A **tax deduction for the cost of business assets**, spread over time instead of deducting the full cost in one year.

**Example:**
A restaurant buys kitchen equipment for **₦2,000,000**.
Instead of deducting ₦2,000,000 immediately, the tax system allows the cost to be **deducted gradually over several years**.

---

# Personal Reliefs

## Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA)
This is the **main tax relief most Nigerian employees get automatically**. It reduces the amount of your income that will be taxed.

In Nigeria, CRA is usually calculated as:
- **₦200,000 OR 1% of gross income (whichever is higher)**
- **PLUS 20% of your gross income**

### Practical Example
Chinedu works in Lagos and earns:
- Salary: ₦350,000 per month
- Yearly income: ₦4,200,000

CRA calculation:
- 1% of income = ₦42,000
- Minimum allowed = ₦200,000 → use ₦200,000
- 20% of income = ₦840,000

Total CRA: ₦200,000 + ₦840,000 = **₦1,040,000**

So instead of paying tax on ₦4,200,000, he pays tax on:
₦4,200,000 − ₦1,040,000 = **₦3,160,000**

👉 That reduction is the **CRA benefit**.

---

## Personal Relief Allowance
This used to be a **separate fixed allowance for individuals**, but under modern Nigerian tax rules it has mostly been **absorbed into the Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA)**.

### Practical Meaning for Nigerians
If you are already claiming **CRA**, you usually **do not need to enter a separate Personal Relief Allowance**.

So for most taxpayers:
- **CRA already covers personal relief**

---

## Children Allowance
This relief applies when you **financially support your children**.

Typical criteria include:
1. The child must be **your biological or legally adopted child**
2. The child is **usually under 18** (or still in school)
3. The child **depends on you financially**
4. The child is **not earning significant income**

### Practical Example
Ada has:
- 2 children in secondary school
- She pays their school fees and feeding

She may claim **Children Allowance** for those two dependents.

### What documents help if requested
Usually tax authorities may ask for:
- Birth certificates
- School fee receipts
- Evidence the child depends on you

Many PAYE employees **never need to submit documents unless audited**.

---

## Dependent Relatives
This relief applies when you **financially support relatives who cannot support themselves**.

Typical relatives include:
- Parents
- Grandparents
- Siblings
- Sometimes extended family members

But the key requirement is that they **depend on you financially**.

### Practical Example
Emeka works in Abuja and sends:
- ₦40,000 monthly to his retired mother
- ₦25,000 monthly to support his younger sister in university

These relatives qualify as **dependent relatives** because:
- They rely on his financial support
- They have little or no income

### Can you claim deductions for money you send?
Yes — **if the tax system allows dependent relative relief**.

But typically:
- There is a **maximum allowed relief amount per dependent**
- You **cannot claim unlimited deductions**

### What documents might be required?
Usually only if the tax authority asks.

Possible proof includes:
- Bank transfers
- Receipts of school fees
- Evidence of relationship
- Proof the relative has little or no income

In practice, many Nigerians **just declare it honestly unless asked for verification**.

---

## Life Assurance
You can claim relief for **life insurance premiums you pay**.

### Example
Ngozi pays: ₦15,000 monthly life insurance premium
Yearly payment: ₦180,000

She can claim **₦180,000 as life assurance relief**.

Proof usually includes:
- Insurance policy document
- Payment receipts

---

## National Housing Fund (NHF)
Employees often contribute **2.5% of their salary** to the NHF.

### Example
Salary: ₦200,000 monthly
NHF contribution: 2.5% = ₦5,000 monthly
Yearly NHF contribution: ₦60,000

That **₦60,000 can reduce taxable income**.

---

## National Pension Scheme
Employees typically contribute **8% of salary to pension**.

### Example
Salary: ₦300,000 monthly
Pension deduction: ₦24,000 monthly
Yearly pension contribution: ₦288,000

That amount **reduces taxable income**.

---

## Other Reliefs
This field allows **any additional legally approved tax relief not already listed**.

### Practical Examples Nigerians may see

**1️⃣ Disability Relief**
If a taxpayer has a **certified disability**, tax law may allow additional relief.

Example: A worker with a certified disability may claim an extra deduction.

Documents required:
- Medical certificate
- Disability verification

---

**2️⃣ Approved Charitable Donations**
Donations to **government-approved charities** may sometimes qualify.

Example: Donating ₦200,000 to an approved medical foundation.

Proof required:
- Donation receipt
- Evidence the organization is approved

---

**3️⃣ Government-approved relief programs**
Sometimes the government introduces **special reliefs for certain sectors or situations**.

Example:
- Agricultural support relief
- SME incentives

---

# Withholding Tax (WHT)
**Withholding Tax (WHT)** is **tax deducted at the source before you receive payment**.
The person or company paying you **deducts the tax and sends it to the government on your behalf**.
When filing taxes, you can **declare these deductions so they count toward the tax you already paid**.

---

## WHT on Rent
Tax deducted from **income earned from renting out property**.

**Example:**
You rent out a shop in Surulere for **₦2,000,000 per year**.
The tenant is a company, and they deduct **10% WHT** before paying you.
- Total rent: ₦2,000,000
- WHT deducted: ₦200,000
- Amount you receive: ₦1,800,000

That **₦200,000 becomes tax already paid**.

---

## WHT on Commission
Tax deducted from **commission income**.

**Example:**
A real estate agent helps a company sell land and earns **₦500,000 commission**.
The company deducts **10% WHT**.
- Commission: ₦500,000
- WHT deducted: ₦50,000
- Amount received: ₦450,000

The **₦50,000 counts as tax already paid**.

---

## WHT on Consultancy / Professional Service
Tax deducted from **professional services or consulting work**.

Common professions include:
- Software developers
- Lawyers
- Accountants
- Engineers
- Doctors

**Example:**
A software developer is paid **₦1,000,000** to build a website.
The client deducts **10% WHT**.
- Service fee: ₦1,000,000
- WHT deducted: ₦100,000
- Amount received: ₦900,000

The developer can **claim the ₦100,000 when filing taxes**.

---

## WHT on Directors Fee
Tax deducted from **fees paid to company directors**.

**Example:**
A company pays a board member **₦2,000,000 director's fee**.
They deduct **10% WHT**.
- Fee: ₦2,000,000
- WHT deducted: ₦200,000
- Amount received: ₦1,800,000

The **₦200,000 is tax already paid**.

---

## WHT on Contract
Tax deducted from **payments for contracts or project work**.

This is common for:
- Construction projects
- Supply contracts
- IT projects
- Renovation work

**Example:**
A contractor is paid **₦5,000,000** to renovate an office.
The company deducts **5% WHT**.
- Contract value: ₦5,000,000
- WHT deducted: ₦250,000
- Amount received: ₦4,750,000

The **₦250,000 becomes tax credit**.

---

## WHT Others
This covers **any withholding tax not listed above**.

Examples may include:
- WHT on royalties
- WHT on management fees
- WHT on agency services

**Example:**
A content creator licenses a course to a company for **₦300,000**.
The company deducts **₦30,000 WHT** before payment.

---

# Tax Returns

## Statement of Income - Gross Annual Income
This section shows **all the money you made in the year before any deductions**.
You are expected to **upload documents** to support each income type.

---

### Documents
Files that prove the income you earned.

**Examples:**
- Salary slips
- Bank statements
- Contracts
- Invoices

---

### Name
The **type of income** you are declaring.

---

### Upload File
Where you **attach proof of that income**.

---

## Income Types

### Salary
Money earned from working for a company.

**Example:**
₦500,000 monthly salary from your employer.

**Document:**
- Payslip
- Employment letter
- Bank statement

---

### Commission
Money earned from **selling something or closing deals**.

**Example:**
A real estate agent earning ₦300,000 commission.

---

### Trade Income
Profit from running a **business or selling goods/services**.

**Example:**
A shop owner making ₦2,000,000 yearly profit.

---

### Allowance
Extra payments received apart from salary.

**Examples:**
- Transport allowance
- Housing allowance
- Meal allowance

---

### Pension
Money received after retirement **or pension contributions**.

**Example:**
Monthly pension payments or pension deductions.

---

### Annuity
Regular payments received from an **investment or insurance plan**.

**Example:**
A retiree receiving fixed yearly payments from an annuity plan.

---

### Gratuities
A **one-time payment** received after long service or retirement.

**Example:**
₦3,000,000 paid after 20 years of work.

---

### Foreign Income
Income earned from **outside Nigeria**.

**Example:**
Remote job payments from a UK or US company.

---

### Dividend
Money earned from **owning shares in a company**.

**Example:**
Dividends received from shares in **Dangote Cement**.

---

### Interest
Money earned from **savings or investments**.

**Example:**
Interest from a bank account or treasury bills.

---

### Rent
Money earned from **renting out property**.

**Example:**
₦1,500,000 yearly rent from a tenant.

---

### Other Income(s)
Any income not listed above.

**Example:**
- Side hustle income
- Gifts related to business
- One-time payments

👉 You can add multiple entries.

# Mandatory Disclosure of Accommodation
This section is **about where you live**. It is required.

---

### Address
The **full address of your residence**.

---

### Accommodation Type
Type of building you live in.

**Examples:**
- Flat
- Duplex
- Self-contained

---

### Ownership Type
Who owns the property:
- **Tenant** → You rent the property
- **Landlord** → You own the property
- **Others** → e.g. living with family

---

### Owner Name
Name of the landlord or property owner.

---

### Owner Payer ID
Tax ID of the property owner (if known).

---

### Owner Address
Address of the landlord.

---

### Rent Paid
Total rent you paid for the year.

**Example:**
₦1,200,000 yearly rent

---

### Rent Paid By Employer
Indicates if your company paid your rent.
- If yes, enter the amount
- If no, leave blank or 0.00

---

### Date Started
When you started living in the property.

---

### Date End
When you stopped (if applicable).
